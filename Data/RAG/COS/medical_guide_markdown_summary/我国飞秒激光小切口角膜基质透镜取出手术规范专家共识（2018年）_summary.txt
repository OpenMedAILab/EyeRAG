The "Expert Consensus on the Standardization of Femtosecond Laser Small Incision Lenticule Extraction Surgery in China (2018)" provides updated guidelines for the SMILE procedure, a minimally invasive method for correcting refractive errors like myopia and astigmatism. Developed by the Chinese Medical Association's Ophthalmology Group, the consensus outlines requirements for medical institutions, practitioner qualifications, and patient criteria, including age and health conditions. It emphasizes the need for thorough preoperative assessments, informed consent, and detailed surgical protocols. Additionally, it addresses indications, contraindications, and potential complications, aiming to enhance the safety and effectiveness of SMILE surgery while promoting adherence to best practices.
