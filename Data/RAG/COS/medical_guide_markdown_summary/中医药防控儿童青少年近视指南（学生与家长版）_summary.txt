The "Guidelines for the Prevention and Control of Myopia in Children and Adolescents" by the Chinese Association of Traditional Chinese Medicine address the alarming rise of myopia, affecting nearly 54% of youth in China. They advocate for a collaborative effort among government, schools, families, and society to tackle this public health issue. The guidelines incorporate traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) perspectives, offering prevention and treatment methods such as acupuncture, herbal remedies, and dietary changes. Key recommendations include regular vision screenings, good eye hygiene, limited screen time, increased outdoor activities, and a healthy lifestyle. The guidelines aim to educate parents and children about myopia risk factors and emphasize the importance of professional medical advice.
