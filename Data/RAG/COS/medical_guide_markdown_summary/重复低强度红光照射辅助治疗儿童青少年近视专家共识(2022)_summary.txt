The "Expert Consensus on Repeated Low-Intensity Red Light Therapy for Myopia in Children and Adolescents" provides guidelines for using this treatment to slow myopia progression in young patients. While initial studies show promise, inconsistent application and lack of standardized protocols raise concerns. The consensus covers treatment principles, candidate selection, dosage, frequency, equipment, potential adverse reactions, and integration with other therapies. It stresses the need for careful monitoring and adherence to guidelines for safety and efficacy, while also highlighting the necessity for further research on long-term outcomes.
