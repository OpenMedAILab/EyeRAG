The Ophthalmology Branch of the China Association of Traditional Chinese Medicine has developed a consensus on the use of Radix Isatidis eye drops for treating bacterial conjunctivitis. These eye drops, derived from traditional Chinese medicine, are effective in treating bacterial ocular infections without contributing to antibiotic resistance. The consensus details their indications, benefits, dosage, side effects, and contraindications, highlighting their ability to relieve symptoms like redness and discomfort. It also suggests their use alongside antibiotics for improved outcomes. The document aims to guide clinicians in safe and standardized application while advocating for further research to support evidence-based practices.
