The document presents a consensus on improving interventions to prevent myopia in children and adolescents in China, addressing its rising prevalence as a public health concern. It advocates for a comprehensive approach that includes behavior modification, increased outdoor activity, and collaboration between families and schools. Key strategies involve establishing a myopia warning system, monitoring eye use, and providing educational resources. The consensus emphasizes cost-effective, safe measures, early intervention, and standardized public health messaging to enhance understanding and engagement. The overall goal is to reduce myopia incidence and progression, improving eye health among the youth in China.
