The "Chinese Consensus on the Selection of Indications for Minimally Invasive Glaucoma Surgery (2023)" emphasizes the growing significance of minimally invasive glaucoma surgery (MIGS) in China. Developed by the Glaucoma Group of the Chinese Medical Association, it aims to standardize MIGS classification, indications, and management practices to enhance clinical outcomes and minimize complications. The consensus categorizes MIGS by mechanisms and approaches, outlines specific procedural indications, and stresses the importance of preoperative assessments and ongoing research to improve techniques and patient care.
