The "Expert Consensus on Electronic Medical Records for Ophthalmology Outpatients (2024)" was created by the Chinese Medical Information and Big Data Association to address the limitations of standard outpatient EMRs for ophthalmology. It aims to standardize diagnosis and treatment, improve record efficiency, monitor chronic eye diseases, and aid clinical decision-making. The consensus advocates for a customized EMR system that meets ophthalmic needs, incorporating specific data requirements and ensuring compliance with regulations and standards. It emphasizes data security, quality control, and the potential for advancements in telemedicine. This document serves as a guide for designing and managing ophthalmic EMRs to enhance healthcare quality and efficiency.
