The "Expert Consensus on Dry Eye Related to Eye Surgery" (2021) highlights the significant impact of dry eye as a common complication following ophthalmic surgeries. It outlines the condition's definition, risk factors, pathogenesis, clinical features, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention strategies, emphasizing the need for effective management to improve surgical outcomes. Key findings indicate that the incidence of dry eye varies based on surgical type, patient age, and pre-existing conditions. Recommendations include early intervention, addressing risk factors, and utilizing both non-pharmacological and pharmacological therapies to standardize practices and enhance clinical management of dry eye.
