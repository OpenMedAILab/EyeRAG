A consensus among experts in Shanghai highlights the importance of low-concentration atropine sulfate eye drops for managing myopia in children and adolescents. Due to the lack of commercially available formulations in China, hospital-prepared solutions are used without standardized protocols, raising safety and efficacy concerns. The guidelines emphasize strict preparation standards, including pH, osmotic pressure, and preservatives, particularly for pediatric use. The document also addresses dosage forms, stability, and storage conditions, aiming to guide ophthalmologists and pharmacists in the safe and effective preparation of these eye drops.
