The "Chinese Low Vision Rehabilitation Clinical Guideline (2021)" provides a systematic framework for diagnosing, assessing, and rehabilitating individuals with low vision in China. Developed by experts, it emphasizes standardized practices to improve patients' independence and quality of life. Key components include defining low vision based on best corrected visual acuity, identifying common causes, recommending comprehensive assessments, proposing a multifaceted rehabilitation approach, and highlighting the importance of ongoing follow-up. The guideline serves as a resource for healthcare professionals to enhance care for low vision patients and acknowledges the need for regular updates in the field.
