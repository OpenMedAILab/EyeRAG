The "Expert Consensus for the Operating Procedures for Bacterial Examination of Ocular Infection (2019)" updates guidelines for microbiological examination of eye infections, enhancing the 2015 version. Key revisions include expanded pathogen information, revised reporting formats, updated specimen collection and culture procedures, and clarified operational details. The consensus highlights the importance of accurate specimen collection, timely processing, and proper reporting for clinical diagnosis. It provides methods for collecting various ocular specimens, emphasizes contamination prevention, and outlines specimen handling, staining methods, and culture result reporting, while stressing biosecurity protocols for all specimens.
