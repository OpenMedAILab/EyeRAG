The "Chinese Expert Consensus on Telemedicine for Diabetic Retinopathy (2023)" provides a framework for implementing telemedicine in the screening and management of diabetic retinopathy in China. Organized by the Public Health Ophthalmology Branch, it aims to enhance primary healthcare capabilities by establishing standards for telemedicine systems, image quality, diagnostic criteria, and referral processes. The consensus emphasizes the use of advanced technologies like artificial intelligence, the training of healthcare personnel, data security, and a collaborative healthcare model for timely referrals. Its ultimate goal is to improve public health and reduce the burden of diabetic retinopathy in China.
