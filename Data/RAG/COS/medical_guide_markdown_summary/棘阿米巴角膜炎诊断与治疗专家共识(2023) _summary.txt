The "Chinese Expert Consensus on Diagnosis and Treatment of Acanthamoeba Keratitis (2023)" addresses the serious corneal disease caused by Acanthamoeba, which can lead to blindness if not promptly diagnosed and treated. It highlights challenges in recognizing early symptoms, leading to misdiagnosis, and emphasizes the need for standardized diagnostic and treatment protocols. Developed by experts from the Chinese Ophthalmologist Association, the consensus includes recommendations for timely anti-Acanthamoeba medications, monitoring treatment responses, and considering surgical options for severe cases. It outlines the disease's epidemiology, risk factors, clinical manifestations, diagnostic methods, and treatment strategies to improve awareness and clinical practices in China.
