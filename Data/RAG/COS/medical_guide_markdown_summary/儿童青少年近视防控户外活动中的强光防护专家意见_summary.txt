The document "Expert Opinions on Bright Light Protection during Outdoor Activities for Myopia Prevention in Children and Adolescents" underscores the significance of outdoor activities and specialized lenses in preventing myopia in youth while addressing the risks of sunlight exposure. Recommendations from ophthalmology experts include ensuring at least two hours of outdoor activity daily, using protective eyewear, and minimizing intense sunlight exposure. It highlights photochromic lenses as a solution for both myopia control and light protection, especially for children using atropine eye drops. The guidance aims to promote outdoor activities while protecting eye health from UV damage and bright light discomfort.
