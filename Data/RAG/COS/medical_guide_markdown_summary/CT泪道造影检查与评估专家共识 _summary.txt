The "Expert Consensus on CT Dacryocystography Examination and Evaluation" provides standardized protocols for diagnosing and managing lacrimal duct obstruction in ophthalmology. Developed by Chinese medical experts, it covers indications, contraindications, pre-examination preparations, contrast usage, data collection, image processing, and diagnostic criteria. The consensus highlights the need for early diagnosis to prevent complications like corneal ulcers and blindness, and includes recommendations for imaging techniques, quality control, and structured reporting to improve treatment for lacrimal duct diseases. It is registered with an international guidelines platform to ensure evidence-based relevance.
