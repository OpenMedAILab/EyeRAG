The "Chinese Expert Consensus on the Diagnosis and Management of Infectious Endophthalmitis After Ophthalmic Surgery (2022)" addresses the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of infectious endophthalmitis following ophthalmic surgeries. It categorizes the condition into acute and chronic forms, highlighting clinical features, common pathogens, and the importance of timely diagnosis and treatment. The consensus recommends empirical therapy based on clinical presentation and pathogen profiles, with key strategies including specific antibiotics, antifungals, and surgical interventions like vitrectomy. It aims to guide clinicians in improving patient outcomes and calls for further research to enhance recommendations.
