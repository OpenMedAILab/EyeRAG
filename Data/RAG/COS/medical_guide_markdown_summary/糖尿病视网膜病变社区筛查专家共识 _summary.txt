The expert consensus on community screening for diabetic retinopathy (DR) stresses the need for early detection to prevent blindness, as DR is a significant complication of diabetes. It calls for an effective screening model that includes standardized methods, defined personnel roles, proper equipment, and sustainability. With the increasing prevalence of diabetes and DR in China, regular eye exams are advocated to reduce vision loss. The document also highlights the potential of artificial intelligence to improve screening efficiency and outlines a structured process with clear responsibilities and referral standards. Emphasizing quality control and patient education, the consensus aims to enhance DR screening rates and integrate these efforts into China's public health system.
