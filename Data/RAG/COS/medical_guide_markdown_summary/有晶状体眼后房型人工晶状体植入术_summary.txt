The "2024 Guidelines for the Implantation of Posterior Chamber Intraocular Lenses in Phakic Eyes" establish standards for surgery aimed at correcting refractive errors. Key points include:

1. **Environmental Standards**: Compliance with disinfection and equipment requirements.
2. **Surgeon Qualifications**: Surgeons should be qualified ophthalmologists with relevant training.
3. **Preoperative Assessment**: Comprehensive evaluations of patient history and eye health are necessary.
4. **Indications/Contraindications**: Suitable for patients over 18 with stable refractive errors; contraindications include unstable keratoconus and severe dry eye.
5. **Informed Consent**: Patients must be informed about the procedure, risks, and alternatives.
6. **Surgical Procedure**: Detailed steps for preparation, execution, and postoperative care are outlined.
7. **Postoperative Care**: Guidelines for medication, monitoring, and follow-up visits are provided.
8. **Complications Management**: Strategies for managing potential complications are included.

The guidelines will be updated as clinical practices evolve.
