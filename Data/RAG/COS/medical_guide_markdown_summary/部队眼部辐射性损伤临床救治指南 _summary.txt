The "Clinical Guidelines for the Treatment of Ocular Radiation Injury in the Army" addresses the risk of radiation damage to the eyes in military personnel, emphasizing the need for standardized diagnosis and treatment protocols due to limited current understanding. It details the effects of both ionizing and non-ionizing radiation on eye tissues, leading to conditions such as photokeratitis, cataracts, and retinal damage. The guidelines recommend strategies for diagnosis, management, and protective measures, underscoring the importance of timely treatment to ensure military readiness and effectiveness.
