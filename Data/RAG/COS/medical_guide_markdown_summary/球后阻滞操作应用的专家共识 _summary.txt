The expert consensus on retrobulbar block in ophthalmology highlights its importance as a standard anesthesia method for intraocular surgeries. This procedure involves injecting local anesthetics behind the eyeball to block nerves, providing pain relief and reducing eye movement and intraocular pressure. Although complications are rare, they can be serious. The consensus covers indications, neuroanatomy, safety, techniques, anesthetics, contraindications, and complication management, stressing the need for careful patient assessment and monitoring to ensure safety and effectiveness.
