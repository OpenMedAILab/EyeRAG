The clinical statement highlights the significance of inpatient ophthalmology consultations in teaching hospitals, where patients may experience ocular symptoms due to other medical issues or trauma. A review of consultation records showed that decreased vision was the most common referral reason, with a notable prevalence of ocular abnormalities in pediatric patients. Urgent conditions like globe trauma, chemical injuries, and retinal detachment were identified as critical reasons for consultation. The statement underscores the need for prompt ophthalmic evaluations to prevent worsening eye conditions and encourages medical staff to involve ophthalmologists when necessary.
