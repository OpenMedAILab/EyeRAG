The American Academy of Ophthalmology emphasizes the need to distinguish between donor organs and ocular tissue under the National Organ Transplant Act. They highlight the successful 50-year history of eye banks in providing corneal tissue and recommend including corneal tissue representatives in federal advisory bodies to safeguard eye banking. Key recommendations include maintaining clear distinctions between organs and tissues, fostering cooperation between eye banks and organ procurement organizations, enhancing quality control, keeping recovery costs reasonable, and protecting recipients from transmissible diseases.
