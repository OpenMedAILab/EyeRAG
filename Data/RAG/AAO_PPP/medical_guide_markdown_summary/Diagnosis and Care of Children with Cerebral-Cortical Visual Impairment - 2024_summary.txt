The clinical report "Diagnosis and Care of Children With Cerebral/Cortical Visual Impairment" identifies cerebral/cortical visual impairment (CVI) as a major cause of visual impairment in children, particularly in developed countries. It stresses the importance of early diagnosis and management for optimal learning and development, noting the challenges in detecting CVI through traditional screenings. A multidisciplinary approach involving various healthcare specialists is essential for effective diagnosis and referral. The report discusses CVI characteristics, risk factors, and the necessity for tailored educational support and early intervention. It advocates for improved communication among providers, equitable care access, and further research, with recommendations for early identification, addressing care disparities, optimizing transition planning for young adults, and enhancing educational resources for professionals.
