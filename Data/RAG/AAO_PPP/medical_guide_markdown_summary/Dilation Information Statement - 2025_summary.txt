Pharmacologic mydriasis using tropicamide 1% significantly improves the diagnostic accuracy of diabetic retinopathy screening via color fundus photography. Concerns about acute angle-closure glaucoma are largely unfounded, as studies show a very low incidence of such cases following dilation. Routine pharmacologic dilation is recommended for diabetic retinopathy screening, while primary care should ensure referral networks for ophthalmological emergencies. Immediate medical attention is necessary for symptoms of acute angle closure.
