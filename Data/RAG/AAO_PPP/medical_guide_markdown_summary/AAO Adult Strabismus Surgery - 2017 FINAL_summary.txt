The joint policy statement from the American Association for Pediatric Ophthalmology and Strabismus and the American Academy of Ophthalmology outlines the goals and indications for adult strabismus surgery, which include restoring ocular alignment and alleviating symptoms like double vision and visual confusion. The statement highlights the negative impact of strabismus on quality of life and emphasizes that successful surgery can improve visual function and psychosocial well-being. Patients are advised to discuss the risks and benefits of surgery with their ophthalmologist, considering alternative treatment options as well.
